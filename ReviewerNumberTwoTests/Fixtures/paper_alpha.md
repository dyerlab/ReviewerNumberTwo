# Gene Flow and Population Structure in Riparian Oaks

DOI: 10.1111/test.0001

## Abstract

Patterns of genetic variation in riparian tree species reflect historical dispersal and contemporary gene flow.

## Introduction

Understanding how genes move through fragmented landscapes is a central question in landscape genetics. Riparian corridors are thought to facilitate movement of propagules across otherwise inhospitable terrain.

Previous work has established that pollen dispersal in oaks can extend several kilometres, but the effective contribution of long-distance events to local gene pools remains uncertain.

In this study we quantify gene flow and fine-scale population structure in valley oak using microsatellite markers across a fragmented river system.

## Methods

Leaf tissue was collected from 240 individuals at 12 sites along the Santa Ana River. Genomic DNA was extracted using a CTAB protocol and amplified at eight microsatellite loci.

## Results

FST values ranged from 0.02 to 0.09 across pairwise site comparisons, indicating moderate genetic differentiation.
