# Isolation by Distance in Marine Invertebrates Along the Pacific Coast

DOI: 10.1111/test.0002

## Abstract

Marine species with planktonic larvae often show weak but significant isolation by distance across oceanic barriers.

## Introduction

Connectivity among marine populations is governed by oceanographic currents, larval duration, and behaviour during the pelagic phase. Quantifying this connectivity is essential for designing effective marine protected area networks.

Empirical studies have produced conflicting evidence for isolation by distance in species with extended larval phases, possibly because sampling designs rarely capture the relevant geographic scale.

Here we test for isolation by distance in three co-distributed invertebrate species using mitochondrial sequence data from 18 sites spanning 1 400 km of coastline.

## Methods

Tissue samples were preserved in 95% ethanol in the field. The COI barcode region was amplified and sequenced for 320 individuals per species.

## Results

Mantel tests revealed significant isolation by distance in two of three species.
